Space Habitat Designer: Interactive Simulator

The Space Habitat Designer project is an interactive tool for simulating and optimizing the design of autonomous living and working modules in extraterrestrial environments. Created to address key challenges in space architecture, our simulator allows engineers, architects, and enthusiasts to design functional and safe space habitats.

Features and Functionality

  • Habitat Selection: You can choose one of several locations (Mars, Moon, ISS, or Transit Module) to adapt your design to the unique conditions of each environment, including gravity and atmospheric features.
  • Modular Approach: Define the primary parameters of your module (length, width, shape, height, and crew size) and operate within realistic structural constraints.
  • Detailed Planning and Zoning: Use an intuitive editor to create functional zones (Crew Quarters, Laboratory, Galley, Hygiene Module, and others). Each zone can be moved, scaled, and filled with necessary equipment.
  • Analytics and Life Support: The system provides real-time critical data, including the calculation of minimum space per person, and warns of the absence of essential zones (e.g., hygiene or medical modules).
  • Project Export: The final design can be exported as a JSON file for further data analysis or as a **PNG** image for presenting ideas.

Space Habitat Designer transforms the complex process of space architecture into an accessible and engaging task, helping to visualize and plan humanity's future beyond Earth.

Data Sources and Methodology

The functionality and analytical checks in Space Habitat Designer are based on official documents and research papers from NASA and its partners.

The following key resources were used for calculations and modeling:

  • Volume and Area Requirements: “Defining the Net Habitable Volume for Long Duration Exploration Missions” (defines metrics, such as minimum volume per crew member, used in the analytics).
  • Concept and Mission: “Moon to Mars Architecture Definition Document” (used to define mission scenarios and functions that the module must support).
  • Design Guidelines: “Deep Space Habitation Design Guidelines” (forms the basis for the logic checking for critically required zones: medical, hygiene, galley).
  • Low Gravity Design: "NASA’s Moon to Mars (M2M) Transit Habitat Refinement Point of Departure Design" (justifies special zone requirements, such as increased area for exercise in weightlessness or low gravity).

These documents provide the scientific basis for ensuring your project is as close as possible to space engineering standards.

The Contribution of Artificial Intelligence (AI)

Advanced Artificial Intelligence (AI) technologies were actively utilized in the development of the **Space Habitat Designer** interactive simulator to enhance code quality and accelerate the development process.

We used large language models, such as **Gemini**, as a powerful tool to **assist in writing, optimizing, and debugging the JavaScript code**. The AI model played a key role in:

  • Generating Complex Algorithms: Used to create core data structures and logic, for example, for calculations within the **"Analytics and Life Support"** module.
  • Performance Optimization: Aided in refactoring and improving the JavaScript code to ensure the simulator runs smoothly and quickly.
  • Documentation Creation: Assisted in writing detailed comments and technical documentation for the functional parts of the project.

Using **Gemini** allowed our team to focus on the complexities of space architecture while the AI ensured a high-quality, clean, and efficient codebase.
